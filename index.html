Puzzle design
All levels are handcrafted and ordered by difficulty, so new players can learn step by step. Early stages teach the basics, mid-game mixes in tighter layouts and traps, and late-game levels are designed to punish lazy thinking and reward careful planning.
Game modes
Classic mode lets you work through all 1000 levels at your own pace, with unlimited undo and restart. In Time Challenge, you get a random sequence of puzzles and race to clear as many as you can before you slip up, competing on a global leaderboard.
Focused experience
Minimal UI, one-hand controls, and a calm background track keep all attention on the puzzle itself. No pop-up spam, no forced sign-ins, and no daily grind systems—just a clean Sokoban experience you can pick up and put down anytime.
Built by one person
Sokoban 1000 Levels is solo-developed over months: every layout, difficulty curve, and update is done by a single dev obsessed with box-pushing puzzles. Future updates will continue to refine level balance, add quality-of-life tweaks, and expand modes without compromising the core design.
